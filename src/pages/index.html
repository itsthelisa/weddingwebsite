Getting There

Ferry

You'll be taking a ferry to get across to Waiheke. It's a half hour trip with a great view of Rangitoto. Ferries run regularly throughout the day and it takes roughly 40 minutes from the Auckland waterfront to Waiheke.

The last ferry back on the night of the wedding is at 12.30am, so you can still party with us and head back that night if you'd prefer not to stay on the island.

The passenger only ferry is run by Fullers and there is a car ferry run by Sealink.

Car

Bringing a car over to Waiheke probably isn't necessary unless you are staying beyond Oneroa and want to explore the island.

If you're planning on bringing your car over to Waiheke you'll need to take the Sealink car ferry, and it's a good idea to book your ticket in advance.

Bus

We will be organising a bus that will go between the ferry terminal, Oneroa and Mudbrick. We will provide more information closer to the time and let you know.

There are also public bus routes on the island that can get you from the ferry to Oneroa.

Where to stay

There are heaps of lovely places to stay on Waiheke. The best place to be location-wise is near Oneroa as we will be organising a bus which will stop in Oneroa to bring guests to and from the wedding venue.

You can find accomodation at hotels and motels or alternatively there are plenty of options with Airbnb, Bookabach or Holiday Houses
